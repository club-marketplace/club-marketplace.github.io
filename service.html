AMNESTY INTERNATIONAL

Student Leaders: Alfonso Perez, William Duanmu

Faculty Sponsor: Dr. Linda Lehmil

Meeting Day: Day 7 Meeting time - Room 606

Anne Frank once said, “How wonderful it is that nobody need wait a single moment before starting to improve the world.” Amnesty International’s mission and goal is to protect human rights worldwide. We envision a world in which every person enjoys all the rights enshrined in the Universal Declaration of Human Rights and other international human rights standards. Amnesty International awaits your involvement and contribution in making our world a better place.

HABITAT FOR HUMANITY

HEIFER CLUB

Student Leaders: Stephanie Hui, Oliver Wan, Timothy Chui

Faculty Sponsor:Ms Yigru McCaughey

Meeting Day: Day 7 Meeting time - Room TBD

Heifer is a nonprofit organization that aims to end world hunger and poverty by providing families in rural China with livestock, seeds, and extensive training to achieve self-reliance. HKIS Heifer club aims to raise fund and awareness by engaging in annual events such as Race to Feed, Pumpkin Festival and World’s Fair. With the majority of our fund donated to the victims of the Sichuan earthquake, we spread awareness on Day 512, the anniversary of the earthquake. Heifer works to provide hope and gifts to families in need.

UNICEF

Student Leaders:Jacinta Chen, Christopher Shim, Sharon Hayon Kim and Kwan Pok Wong

Faculty Sponsor: Mrs. Yingru McCaughey

Meeting Day: Day 1 Meeting time - Room 605/606

Service Requirements: 6 hours per semester for membership (12 hours by the end of the year for year-long members) 20 hours by the end of the year for leadership eligibility

We “think globally, act locally” by advocating for protection of children’s rights, responding to global issues to provide relief for children, promoting equal rights for girls and minorities and working toward peace and social progress. We raise awareness through events like the Stanley Performance, fundraise through bake sales, World’s Fair and Pumpkin Festival, participate in the UNICEF Charity Run, and regularly teach local children English through organizations like Caritas and Oi Kwan. We’re planning a new event this year, which will focus on teaching primary school students about issues children face worldwide and how UNICEF addresses them through games and simulations, while also raising money for the organization, so we would love for you to join us!

Events:

Stanley Performance

UNICEF Disney Charity Run

Weekly Caritas English Tutoring Sessions

Monthly Oi Kwan English Tutoring Sessions

Monthly Bake Sales

Booths at World’s Fair and Pumpkin Festival

New 2nd Semester Event!

ZONTA CLUB

Student Leaders: Varshika Kanthadai, Helena Lee

Faculty Sponsor: Ms Tracey Cheung

Meeting Day: Day 5 Meeting time - Room 103

Initially established by the auspices of Zonta Club in Hong Kong, Z Club in HKIS aims to serve the local community of Hong Kong through fundraising events, service events, monthly sales, and awareness raising. Every year, Z Club organizes Dinner In The Dark, a blindfolded dinner, for ORBIS, and Encore, a singing contest that fundraises for Christian Action. Z Club's voluntary services range from teaching English, to flag days, to soup kitchen visits.

There are no prerequisites for Z Club. Though, we ask that club members complete 8 service hours per semester though attending service events, helping out with selling tickets, and attending or helping with big events such as Dinner in the Dark and Encore.

Email Sarah Fong at 180632@hkis.edu.hk if you would like to join!
